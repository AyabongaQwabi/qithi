Vabantu Silimela
At sunset, many people come to Qiti. The first Qiti person to arrive here is Ngcongolo and his brother Mafilika. They arrived and stood on the red wall on top of the white hill where the scythes are buried. They were removed by the scythes when they were being chased by the whites in this land of the Gwatyu and crossed over to Qiti land. History says that they came from Cofimvaba in the Shobeni. They said that their capital was being sought and they brought Khethelo and his brother Usdlwengu with them to the light on the top of the cacadu. I come from Congolo, a reed that was made of stone and we are born on the right. The eldest is Mfeketho who was followed by Malithi and Masimini is not the same here in this village because history says that when he gave birth to a child he changed his life and it seems that he can be found in this land of the Frestata, especially Silimela, who I come from. His eldest son is Nkqwiliso. Nkqwiliso left his bride to look for work and never returned. I am just describing him for you, but in the place where he was, he left children, so you know there is a generation of Nkqwiliso's brothers. After him is Tomsana, whom they love and call Ntamenkunizi, then Makhaya, some call Faloni, and finally Mnqayi, some call Daloni, who is the one I am descended from. Umnqayi fathered Thiyekile, his eldest son was Teyimani, who fathered me.

The eldest son of Khethelo was Mbukushe, and that is where they established the village of Mbukushe. He fathered four sons: Zalikho, Galada, Sinoko, and Libaziso.
