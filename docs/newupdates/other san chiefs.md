Based on the sources, here is an enriched list of the San chiefs and captains active during the 19th century, categorized by their primary regions and including details regarding their leadership, alliances, and historical fates.

### **Glen Grey (Lady Frere) and the White Kei Basin**
This region was a critical sanctuary where San leaders negotiated survival through complex alliances with the Thembu kingdom.

*   **Madolo (also Madura or Madoor):** 
    *   **Leadership & Territory:** Chief of a powerful tribe that originally occupied the country around the Klipplaats and Upper Black Kei Rivers. He retired to Glen Grey around 1835 and settled at the **Bushman School** mission on the White Kei (Cacadu) River in 1839.
    *   **Military Service:** In 1846, he led a force of **200 levies** (San, Khoe, Mfengu, and Thembu) to assist the British against the Xhosa in the War of the Axe.
    *   **Fate:** Following a conflict with the Thembu chief Ndhela and colonial authorities in 1850, he fled to caves along the White Kei. He was last reported in 1856, at age 80, retreating into the Maloti-Drakensberg.
*   **Mqithi (or Qithi):**
    *   **Status:** Recognized as the **"Royal San"** leader at Rodana and a primary ally of **Queen Nonesi**.
    *   **Significance:** His lineage held a rank comparable to a senior counselor or hereditary ritual specialist, and his clan was granted land at **Qithi Village** directly adjacent to the Thembu royal residence.
*   **Flux:**
    *   **Actions:** A close associate of Madolo who attacked the immigrant Thembu chief **Ndhela** around 1850.
    *   **Fate:** Forced to flee to the White Kei river caves alongside Madolo to escape colonial arrest.
*   **Mada’kane:**
    *   **Territory:** Chief of a tribe inhabiting the valley of the Lower Black Kei to its junction with the White Kei.
    *   **Stronghold:** His final retreat was an **inaccessible glen** bearing his name near the junction of the two Keis. His brother, **‘Gcu-wa**, was a noted painter still active in 1869.

### **Maloti-Drakensberg and the Phuti Alliance**
The San in this region maintained the most enduring indigenous alliances, particularly with the Phuthi.

*   **Soai:**
    *   **Leadership:** The last major leader of the Maloti San, based at **Sehonghong Cave**.
    *   **Alliances:** A very close friend of the Phuthi chief **Moorosi**, with whom his people frequently intermarried. 
    *   **Fate:** Killed in 1871–1872 by Sotho forces under Joel. He attempted to hide in a deep pool, breathing through a reed, but was spotted and shot.
*   **Quu:** 
    *   **Leadership:** Lived at "Nkuakhomo" during the Difaqane.
    *   **Alliances:** Formalized an alliance with the Phuthi when Chief **Mokuoane** (Moorosi's father) married his sister. He was also related by marriage to the San groups befriended by **Moshoeshoe** at Qeme Mountain.
*   **Mphaki:**
    *   **Background:** A Sotho man raised by the San, he became a San leader and contemporary of Soai.
    *   **Fate:** Forced to move from Qhoasing after his followers raided cattle belonging to Moorosi’s brother.

### **Nomansland (East Griqualand) and the Transkei**
Leaders in this region often balanced a nomadic hunter-gatherer existence with roles as vassals or ritual specialists for the Mpondomise and Bhaca.

*   **Mdwebo:**
    *   **Territory:** Led a band that roamed between the Ngeli Mountains and the Mzimvubu River.
    *   **Alliances:** A vassal of the Mpondomise chief **Mandela**, to whom he was related by marriage.
    *   **Lifestyle:** Despite his political links, his band maintained a nomadic hunter-gatherer appearance and construction of "impromptu" huts.
*   **Nqabayo (also Ngqabayi):**
    *   **Leadership:** Led a band of 43 men at the base of the Drakensberg near the Xuka and Mooi Rivers. He was distinguished by having **two wives** and controlling the preparation of arrow poison.
    *   **Fate:** Fell out with Thembu chief **Mgudhluwa** in 1857 after a horse theft; his band was surrounded and attacked at **Gubenxa**, resulting in the death of most of his men.
*   **Biligwana:** 
    *   **Band:** Leader of the **Thola**, a large, heavily creolised band of 200 San, Khoe, and runaway servants. 
    *   **Military:** His followers were notable for being **armed with guns**.
*   **Luhayi:** 
    *   **Role:** Head of a main San family in the Tsolo district and the **official rainmaker** for the Mpondomise chief Mditshwa.
    *   **Cultural Legacy:** He was photographed by Stanford in 1888. His family occupied a liminal position, performing the role of traditional "doctors" while maintaining San ancestry.
*   **Qangi:** An old chief whose band frequently operated in conjunction with Mdwebo’s followers.

### **Northern Region and the Orange Free State**
*   **Khausob (alias Skeelkobus):** 
    *   **Leadership:** Paramount San chief of the entire country between the Vaal, Modder, and Riet Rivers. 
    *   **Conflict:** Embittered by the loss of his land to Boers without payment, he launched raids during **Senekal’s War** (1858).
    *   **Fate:** Killed in a fierce fight when his kraal was encircled by a commando.
*   **'Twa-'goup:** A great San captain of the Modder and Riet Rivers who was **killed by a lion** while hunting in the days of the elder Bloem. He was the father of **'Kousopp**.
*   **'Koroko (or 'Co-ro-ko):** The last paramount chief of the San of the **Jammerberg** ('Kouwe).
*   **'Ma'ko-on:** The "chief captain of all the Malalarene clans," described as a clever man whose people were peaceable. He met with missionary John Campbell in 1820.
*   **Quonsop and Deqoi (or Deqai):** Independent San leaders whose groups were eventually assimilated into the Taung under Chief **Moletsane**.
