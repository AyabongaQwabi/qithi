This guide to animal anatomy in **Xhosa (isiXhosa)** is based strictly on the provided sources, primarily the *Dictionary of the Xhosa Language* by Kropf and Godfrey. It details the body parts of animals typically involved in slaughtering, such as cattle (**inkomo**), sheep (**igusha**), and game (**inyamakazi**).

### **External Anatomy: The Frame of the Beast**

The sources provide precise names for the external parts of the animal, many of which carry significance in identification and traditional marking.

*   **Head and Neck:** The head is **intloko**. The horns are **iimpondo**. A notable feature of cattle is the **ubilo** (dewlap). The throat is known as **umqala**, and the windpipe is **ubhongwana** or **uqhoqhoqho**.
*   **The Trunk:** The back is **umhlana**. The spine or backbone is **umqolo** or **umnyele**. The ribs are generally called **iimbambo**, with the lowest or "false" ribs known as **intlala**.
*   **Limbs and Extremities:** The foreleg is the **umkhono**, while the hindleg is the **umlenze**. The hoof is known as **inqina** or **impupho**. The tail is the **umsila**, which ends in the **itshoba** (tail brush).

### **Internal Anatomy: The Innards (*Izibilini*)**

The internal organs, collectively called **izibilini**, are described with high specificity in the context of butchering.

*   **Vital Organs:** The heart is the **intliziyo**, protected by the **isiphaphu** (pericardium). The lungs and liver are sometimes referred to collectively as **ipapu**, though the liver specifically is **isibindi**. The milt or spleen is **ubende**. The gall or bile is **inyongo**, and the bladder is **isinyi** and  the spleen known as **udakada**.
*   **The Digestive System:** Ruminants like cattle and sheep have complex stomachs. The whole stomach is the **isisu**. The paunch or tripe is **ulusu**. The third stomach (manyplies) is the **isandlwane**, and the lowest stomach is the **injeke**.
*   **Intestines and Related Parts:** Intestines are **amathumbu**. The large intestine is **uqumbu** or **ubolobolo**. The rectum is known as **undonci**. Terms for the colon include **iduntso** and **umnyonkolo**.
*   **Structural Materials:** The diaphragm is known as the **isiqhanga**. Marrow inside the bones is **umongo**, while the specialized oil in bone substance is **umxo**. The partially digested food in the stomach is **umswane**.

### **Tradition and Anatomy of the Cut**

Specific parts of the anatomy are tied to social rank and ritual during slaughter.

*   **Sacred Fat:** The **intlukuhla** (fat on the liver) is the first part offered in a sacrifice to appease ancestors.
*   **Best Cuts:** The **intsonyama** is the choice meat found behind the shoulder, reserved for chiefs or wedding couples. The **incum** (brisket) is also a prized portion.
*   **Hierarchy of the Hindquarter:** The **inxaxeba** (right hindquarter) is a mark of honor sent by a senior chief to a junior chief. The left hindquarter is referred to as the **idikazi**.

### **Anatomy of Small Stock and Fowl**

*   **Birds/Fowl:** The crop of a fowl is the **indlelo**, and the gizzard is the **igila**.
*   **Small Game:** For animals like the **intenetya** (rock hare), the tender flesh near the kidneys is called the **umhlubulo**.
