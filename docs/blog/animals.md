Based on the provided sources, particularly the *Dictionary of the Xhosa Language* by Kropf and Godfrey, here is a compilation of animal names in Xhosa (isiXhosa) with their English translations.

### **Xhosa Animal Lexicon**

This list includes mammals, birds, and other creatures, noting loanwords from other languages where specified in the sources.

#### **Mammals**
*   **Inkomo**: Cattle or Cow.
*   **Igusha**: Sheep (This word is **Khoi/Hottentot** in origin).
*   **Imvu**: Sheep (This is the original **Bantu** root word).
*   **Ihashe**: Horse (Likely derived from a **Khoisan** word for "quagga," though previously thought to be English).
*   **Ibhokhwe**: Goat (Derived from the **Dutch** *bok*).
*   **Imbabala**: Bushbuck.
*   **Ibadi**: Springbuck.
*   **Imbila**: Dassie or Coney.
*   **Imbodla**: African wild cat.
*   **Indlovu**: Elephant.
*   **Ingwe**: Leopard.
*   **Imfene**: Baboon.
*   **Impofu**: Eland (Named for its tawny color).
*   **Inyathi**: Cape Buffalo.
*   **Inqu**: Black Wildebeest or Gnu.
*   **Inyamakazi**: Large wild animals or Antelopes (literally "big meat").
*   **Indlulamithi**: Giraffe (literally "the one higher than the trees").
*   **I-ontyi** or **ihagu**: Domestic pig (derived from **Dutch**).
*   **Inxala**: Rooi rhebok.
*   **Inkamela**: Camel (derived from **English**).
*   **Inja**: Dog.
*   **Inkwili**: Cape hunting dog.
*   **Inqawa**: Lynx or Caracal.
*   **Inzola**: Blesbok.
*   **Isilo**: Any wild or "unclean" animal not used for food.

#### **Birds**
*   **Intaka**: Bird (generic term).
*   **Inkuku**: Domestic fowl or Hen.
*   **Ifubesi**: Spotted Eagle owl.
*   **Inkwababa**: African rook.
*   **Inkonjane**: Swallow.
*   **Ukhozi**: Hawk or Eagle.
*   **Isikhwenene**: Red-shouldered parrot.
*   **Ilowe**: Egyptian goose.
*   **Intsikizi**: Ground hornbill.
*   **I-ungulo** or **i-xhalanga**: Vulture.

#### **Reptiles, Insects, and Others**
*   **Unwabu**: Chameleon (named for its slow walk).
*   **Ingwenya**: Crocodile.
*   **Inyoka**: Snake (generic term).
*   **Inyosi**: Bee.
*   **Imbovane**: Ant.
*   **Intulo**: Land iguana or Lizard.
*   **Uxam**: Water monitor or Iguana.
*   **Inkwane**: A species of termite.

### **Dietary Traditions: What was Eaten (and Forbidden)**

The dietary habits of the Bushmen (San) and the Thembu (and broader Xhosa) differed significantly based on their lifestyles as hunters versus pastoralists.

#### **The Bushmen (San) Diet**
The Bushmen subsisted on what was available in the wild, often eating animals that other tribes avoided.
*   **Game:** They hunted the eland (**impofu**), quagga (**iqwafa**), and elephant (**indlovu**) for meat.
*   **Reptiles:** They regularly ate the land iguana (**intulo**). A specific delicacy was "minced salamander" (**inqweme lentulo**), which the sources note was eaten only by Bushmen or renowned hunters.
*   **Veld Kost:** In times of scarcity, they ate roots, bulbs, insects, lizards, locusts, and even poisonous snakes (after removing the head).
*   **Insects:** They gathered "Bushman rice," which were the chrysalides of white ants, and roasted them.
*   **Ritual Taboos:** They would not give children a jackal’s heart to eat, fearing it would make them timid; instead, they gave them leopard hearts to make them brave.

#### **The Thembu and Xhosa Diet**
For the Thembu, livestock was the foundation of society, and their diet was strictly regulated by social rank and gender.
*   **Cattle and Milk:** Their staple was milk (**amasi**), supplemented by slaughtered cattle (**inkomo**) or sheep (**igusha**).
*   **Hunt Supplements:** While cattle were primary, they also joined in large hunts for leopards, elephants, and bluebuck.
*   **Social Divisions of Meat:**
    *   **Men:** Consumed the brisket (**incuma**), which was considered the best part of the animal.
    *   **Women:** Received the tripe (**ulusu**) and the lowest stomach (**injeke**).
    *   **Chiefs:** The choice meat behind the shoulder (**intsonyama**) and the breast of the eland were reserved for the chief.
*   **Traditional Taboos:**
    *   **Fish:** Traditionally, Xhosa people did not eat fish.
    *   **Elephants:** Because chiefs were identified with elephants, eating them was sometimes viewed as a form of cannibalism.
    *   **Tails:** Girls were forbidden from eating the tails of sheep or cows, as it was believed this would cause the cattle to become restless and scamper off.
    *   **Slaughter Omens:** If an animal did not bellow during ritual slaughter at a marriage, it was considered a bad omen.
